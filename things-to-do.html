Wedding Week Activities

A week of adventure on the Garden Isle — Kauai, Hawaii

The Island
Welcome to Kauai

The oldest and most lush Hawaiian island (and also our favorite one), Kauai offers dramatic coastlines, emerald valleys, and world-class outdoor adventures. We've put together a menu of activities for our wedding week — pick what excites you!

The Napali Coast

Towering sea cliffs, hidden waterfalls, and turquoise waters stretch 17 miles along Kauai's northwest shore.

Sun & Sand
Beaches and Snorkeling

Kauai has incredible beaches on every side of the island. Here are some of our favorites that you'll want to visit during the week.

🏆 Snorkeling at any beach earns 20 pts — See all points

Hanalei Bay

Our favorite beach. Andrew thinks this is the most beautiful place on earth and would happily just go here every day.

Tunnels Beach

Best snorkeling on the north shore. Be warned — there's basically no cell reception here. All the way past Hanalei town.

Anini Beach

Great beach for snorkeling near Princeville. Calm, protected waters thanks to the longest fringing reef in Hawaii.

Poipu Beach

Best beach on the south side of the island for snorkeling, surfing, and bodyboarding.

Sunscreen Warning: Please be careful with what sunscreen you bring to the island! Hawaii only allows mineral-based sunscreen. For the health of the reef ecosystem, please do not bring any sunscreen besides zinc-based sunscreen (no avobenzone, oxybenzone, octisalate, octocrylene, homosalate, etc). If you don't have mineral-based sunscreen at home, just buy it in Hawaii upon arrival.

Water Sports
Surfing at Hanalei Bay
🏆 Earn 45 pts

Gentle, rolling waves and a stunning mountain backdrop make Hanalei Bay one of the best places in Hawaii for beginner and intermediate surfers alike.

OPTION A — GUIDED

Surf Lessons

🏆 45 pts

Professional instructors, board and rash guard included. Perfect for first-timers. About 90 minutes, four people max per instructor. There are a lot of surf schools and surf rental shops in Hanalei — we have previously gone with Hanalei Surf School and Hanalei Surf Co.

OPTION B — DIY

Board Rental

🏆 45 pts

Rent a board from Hanalei Surf Company and paddle out on your own. Soft-tops available for beginners. Full-day and half-day rates. Andrew and Zinnia have done this many times and can provide advice on which break to go to in Hanalei Bay! Surfing in Hanalei Bay is Andrew's favorite thing to do in Hawaii.

Land Adventures
Hiking Waimea Canyon

Known as the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific," Waimea Canyon stretches 14 miles long and 3,600 feet deep. Three hikes offer something for every fitness level — all accessed via Highway 550 through Waimea Canyon & Koke'e State Park. This is around a 1.5-hour drive from Princeville, so any adventure to Waimea Canyon is likely to be a full-day activity.

The Grand Canyon of the Pacific

Red-earth cliffs, misty ridgelines, and views that stretch to the ocean. The drive to the trailheads includes many lookouts, and you should absolutely stop at them on the way there because the views are incredible.

MODERATE — ~4 MILES

Waipo'o Falls

🏆 40 pts

A rewarding out-and-back to a stunning 800-foot waterfall with canyon views the entire way. You can jump in the river in the pool at the turnaround point if it's a hot day. Great for most fitness levels. Allow about 3 hours. Andrew and Zinnia do this hike basically every time they are in Kauai.

STRENUOUS — ~8-9 MILES

Awa'awapuhi Trail

🏆 55 pts

A challenging ridge trail ending at a jaw-dropping overlook 2,500 feet above the Napali Coast. Steep switchbacks on the return. Allow 5–6 hours. Andrew and Zinnia have done this once and it was beautiful, though the uphill on the way back leaves you tired at the end.

STRENUOUS — ~10-12 MILES

Alakai Swamp Trail

🏆 65 pts

Boardwalks wind through one of Earth's wettest spots — a high-altitude cloud-forest bog. Otherworldly landscapes and rare birdlife. A full-day commitment. Andrew and Zinnia haven't done this one yet, but have heard great things about it.

Land Adventures
Hiking the Napali Coast

The Kalalau Trail begins at Ke'e Beach on the north shore and follows the rugged Napali Coast. Two options let you choose your level of adventure — both reward you with some of the most breathtaking scenery in Hawaii.

MODERATE — ~4 MILES

Kalalau Trail to Hanakapi'ai Beach

🏆 40 pts

A stunning 2-mile (each way) coastal hike from Ke'e Beach to the remote Hanakapi'ai Beach. The trail hugs the cliffs with dramatic ocean views the entire way. Moderate difficulty with some elevation changes. Allow about 2–3 hours round trip.

STRENUOUS — ~8 MILES

Kalalau Trail to Hanakapi'ai Falls

🏆 55 pts

Continue past Hanakapi'ai Beach another 2 miles inland through a lush valley to reach the spectacular 300-foot Hanakapi'ai Falls. The trail crosses the stream multiple times and can be slippery. Allow 5–7 hours round trip. A truly unforgettable experience.

Permit Required: You need a permit from the Hawaii DLNR to hike the Kalalau Trail. Permits can be booked online and fill up quickly, so plan ahead!

Underwater
Scuba Diving

Crystal-clear waters, sea turtles, monk seals, and vibrant reef life. All dives through Fathom Five Divers — our favorite dive company and one we've gone with many times — with three experience levels to choose from.

South Shore Scuba Diving

Kauai's south shore offers world-class reef diving with regular sightings of turtles, eels, octopuses, and tropical fish of every variety.

BEGINNER — NO CERTIFICATION

Try Scuba

🏆 65 pts

No certification needed. Pool training followed by a shallow guided dive. There are at most four students per instructor. It's quite fun and not scary, and this Try Scuba class is how Zinnia convinced Andrew to get certified. This class is most likely a 2-tank shore dive at Koloa Landing. Half-day experience.

CERTIFIED DIVERS — 2-TANK BOAT DIVE

2-Tank Boat Dive

🏆 50 pts

Two dives at Kauai's top south-shore sites. Includes gear, weights, and experienced dive masters. Typical depths 40–60 feet. Our favorite dive site might be Sheraton Caverns, where you can frequently see sleeping turtles and dive through arches.

ADVANCED — 3-TANK EXPEDITION

Ni'ihau 3-Tank Expedition

🏆 80 pts

Full-day boat trip to the "Forbidden Island." Three dives in pristine, rarely-visited waters. Large pelagics, Hawaiian monk seals, and dramatic visibility. Advanced certification required. Zinnia saw a seal here!

Water Sports
E-Foiling
🏆 Earn 55 pts

Glide above the water on an electric hydrofoil — it's like flying. No waves or wind needed. Sessions are 90–120 minutes with a max of 3 students per instructor. If your group is larger than 3, you'll do consecutive lessons so everyone still gets plenty of water time.

Fly Above the Water

Most people are up and foiling within 30 minutes. Providers: Kauai Foil Experience & e-Foil Kauai.

Epic Adventure
Napali Coast Kayak
🏆 Earn 100 pts — highest point activity!

The ultimate Kauai adventure: paddle 17 miles along the towering Napali Coast from Ha'ena to Polihale. A full-day journey (roughly 6-7 hours of active paddling) with sea caves, waterfalls, dolphins, and some of the most dramatic coastline on Earth.

This adventure sounds more intimidating than it is. We did this and, while we were very tired by the end and it was definitely demanding, we didn't feel like it was extremely difficult. It is no harder than any other all day kayaking trip; the current and wind is with you and helps a lot.

17 Miles of Paradise

Paddle beneath 4,000-foot sea cliffs, past hidden beaches and into sea caves carved over millennia.

Seasickness Warning: This is open-ocean kayaking and can have swells. To avoid seasickness, you should take Dramamine or use a seasickness patch, even if you usually do not have any trouble with seasickness. Better safe than sorry with this one. If you're not sure, ask us!

Water Adventures
Shorter Kayak Trips (Event Captains Needed)

Not ready for the full Napali expedition? Kauai has beautiful, mellow river kayaking that's perfect for a relaxed half-day on the water.

EASY — HALF DAY

Wailua River (Event Captains Needed)

🏆 35 pts

Paddle up Kauai's longest navigable river through lush jungle to a short trail leading to a hidden waterfall. Calm, flat water — great for all skill levels. This is around 30 min drive to the south, close to Lihue.

EASY — 2-3 HOURS

Hanalei River (Event Captains Needed)

🏆 35 pts

Kayak or stand-up paddleboard along the scenic Hanalei River, framed by taro fields and dramatic mountain ridges. A peaceful, easygoing paddle right in the heart of Hanalei. This is in Hanalei right next to Wishing Well Shave Ice. We recommend stopping for shave ice before or after your kayak.

Community Organized
More Adventures (Event Captains Needed)

There are a lot more things you could do that we haven't planned for. If any of these seem exciting, we'd love for volunteers to organize these activities for themselves and other folks who may be interested. Please add them to the spreadsheet! We have suggestions and info about many of these, so please ask us.

Biking

🏆 30 pts

Ride the Ke Ala Hele Makalae Path starting from Kapa'a Beach Park — a paved coastal trail with gorgeous ocean views the entire way. A very calm and pleasant 2–3 hour biking adventure, perfect for all fitness levels. Zinnia and Andrew do this bike ride pretty much every time they visit Kauai, and it's lovely every time.

Helicopter Tour

🏆 65 pts

See Kauai from above — waterfalls, the Napali Coast, Waimea Canyon, and Mt. Wai'ale'ale (one of the wettest spots on Earth). Doors-off options available. Zinnia and Andrew have done this and really enjoyed it; this is probably one of the best places in the world to see from above.

Deep Sea Fishing

🏆 60 pts

Charter a boat and try for mahi-mahi, ahi tuna, or marlin off the Kauai coast. Half-day and full-day options available for groups.

Botanical Gardens

🏆 30 pts

Explore Kauai's stunning botanical gardens, home to rare tropical plants, towering trees, and gorgeous scenery. A perfect low-key day to soak in the island's natural beauty.

Coffee and Chocolate Farm Tours

🏆 30 pts

Visit a working coffee plantation and taste locally grown chocolate. Learn how Kauai's unique climate produces some of Hawaii's finest coffee and cacao. We've done this on the Big Island, but not yet on Kauai, and we've heard good things about Lydgate Farm.

Zipline & Tubing

🏆 40 pts

Soar over lush valleys on a multi-line zipline course, or float through old sugar plantation irrigation tunnels on an inner tube. We haven't done this on Kauai yet, but it seems fun.
